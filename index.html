Otters of North America

Otters have long been known for their playful nature and adorable habits, making them a favorite among members of the animal kingdom. But otters are still wild animals with distinct characteristics and methods of survival. While most people are generally aware that otters can exist both in freshwater and marine habitats, they may not know that there are actually 13 distinct species of otters. Otters are found all over the world, including in North America, Europe, Asia, South America, and parts of North Africa. Otters are a subfamily of Mustelidae, which means they are most closely related to animals like wolverines, badgers, and weasels.
